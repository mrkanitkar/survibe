# Compliance Check: Survibe Piano App — Instrumental Covers of Popular Hindi Songs

**Date:** March 11, 2026
**Initiative:** Create soundfonts / instrumental piano covers of popular Hindi songs for in-app piano learning
**Market:** India (Indian customers)
**App:** Survibe (Piano Teaching App)

---

## Summary

**Assessment: Proceed with Conditions — Legal Review Strongly Recommended**

Creating instrumental piano covers of copyrighted Hindi songs and distributing them inside the app is legally possible under Indian law, but requires compliance with the Indian Copyright Act. The statutory license mechanism (Section 31C) provides a pathway for cover versions, but comes with strict conditions that may be difficult to satisfy for an app-based distribution model.

---

## Applicable Regulations and Policies

| Regulation / Policy | Relevance | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **Indian Copyright Act, 1957 (Section 31C)** | Directly governs cover versions of musical works | Statutory license with prior notice, royalty payment, 5-year waiting period, limited alterations, same-medium requirement |
| **Indian Copyright Act, 1957 (Sections 14, 51)** | Defines exclusive rights in musical works and infringement | Reproduction and communication of a musical composition requires authorization |
| **Copyright Rules, 2013 (Chapter VII)** | Prescribes procedure for Section 31C compliance | Notice format, royalty payment mechanism, labeling requirements |
| **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act)** | Applies if the app collects user personal data | Consent, purpose limitation, data minimization, security safeguards |
| **Information Technology Act, 2000** | General compliance for digital products in India | Reasonable security practices, intermediary guidelines |
| **Consumer Protection Act, 2019** | Applies to paid in-app content | No misleading claims about content licensing status |

## Key Copyright Analysis

### What Is Being Done (Legally)

Even without using the original sound recording, reproducing the underlying **musical composition** (melody, harmony, structure) is a copyright-regulated act. Under Indian copyright law, the "musical work" (composition) and the "sound recording" are two separate copyrights.

Authorization is needed to use the musical composition — either through:

1. **Direct license** from the music publisher / composer / rights holder, OR
2. **Statutory license** under Section 31C of the Copyright Act

### Section 31C — Statutory License for Cover Versions

| # | Requirement | Status for Survibe | Action Needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **5-year waiting period** — Cover cannot be made until 5 calendar years after the first sound recording | Check each song | Verify original release date. Songs after 2021 cannot be covered under this route. |
| 2 | **Prior notice** — 15 days advance notice to copyright owner and Registrar of Copyrights | Not yet done | File notices for each song. Identify correct rights holders (T-Series, Sony Music India, Zee Music, Tips, Saregama, etc.). |
| 3 | **Advance royalty payment** — Pay royalties at prescribed rate, minimum 50,000 copies per work per calendar year | Not yet done | Calculate royalty obligations. Budget for this. |
| 4 | **No unauthorized alterations** — Cannot alter the musical work beyond what is technically necessary | Potential issue | Instrumental piano arrangement inherently alters the original. May need to argue "technically necessary" or seek direct consent. |
| 5 | **Same medium** — Cover must be in the same medium as the last recording | Potential issue | Soundfont/MIDI inside an app may not match streaming audio. Legal counsel should assess. |
| 6 | **Labeling** — Must clearly state it is a cover version under Section 31C | Easy to comply | Add clear labeling in app UI. |

## Risk Areas

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Copyright infringement claims from music labels | **HIGH** | Strictly comply with Section 31C or obtain direct licenses |
| "Alteration" argument — instrumental arrangement may be unauthorized alteration | **HIGH** | Seek legal opinion; consider direct consent from composers/publishers |
| Medium mismatch — soundfont/MIDI vs streaming audio | **MEDIUM** | Legal counsel should assess |
| 5-year rule violation for recent hits | **HIGH** | Only cover songs released before 2021 under statutory license |
| Royalty miscalculation — 50,000 copy minimum adds up | **MEDIUM** | Budget carefully, start with small catalog |
| App store takedown — rights holders can file claims | **HIGH** | Maintain proof of compliance |
| DPDP Act non-compliance for user data | **MEDIUM** | Implement proper consent and data handling |

---

## Recommended Actions

1. **Engage an Indian IP / entertainment law attorney** before proceeding
2. **Build a song eligibility list** — verify release dates, rights holders, 5-year threshold
3. **Consider direct licensing** from IPRS and major labels as primary route
4. **Start with public domain / royalty-free content** while licensing is in progress
5. **Budget for royalties** — per-song costs under either route
6. **Implement DPDP Act compliance** before May 2027 enforcement deadline
7. **Document everything** — notices, royalties, communications, legal opinions
